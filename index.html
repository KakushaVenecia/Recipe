Beef Pilau Recipe

East African Pilau is a traditional, beautiful fragrant rice dish made with many aromatic spices that adds an amazing depth of flavor to the rice. It can be made with either beef or chicken.
I borrowed this recipe from Kaluhi's Kitchen. Check out her work here

Ingredients for the Pilau Masala

Ingredient Measurement
Black pepper seeds 1 1/2 tablespoons
Whole Clove 2 tablespoons
Whole Cardamom 3 tablespoons
Cinnamon 1 1/2 sticks
Whole Cumin 3 1/4 tablespoons

Ingredients for the Pilau

Ingredient Measurement
Basmati Rice 2 1/2 cups
Large Red Onion 3
Large Potatoes Cubed 4
Minced Garlic Clove 5
Pre-cooked Beef 2 cups
freshly made pilau masala 3 tablespoons

Method For the masala:

  1. Place all the whole spices on an ungreased pan and toast them on low heat for about two minutes until fragrant. The reason why we toast them is so that we can intensify their flavor and release their oils which overall makes for a great masala. You will find all of the individual whole spices from any spice aisle of any well stocked supermarket.
  2. Set aside to cool for a couple of minutes then place them in a grinder and whiz until pulverized. You can grind them in a kinu (pestle & mortar) but it will take longer to pulverize. I like mine abit more of a rough grind but you can make yours alot finer if you prefer.

You can watch the process of making the masala from today’s video here

Method For the Beef Pilau:

  1. Add the onions to the sufuria and cook on low heat until brown. Keep in mind I have said brown not burnt. Make sure you are alert so that they do not burn. Focus my people, focus!!! The darker the onions are, the darker your pilau will be. Pilau gets it’s color from the onions.
  2. Add the potatoes and then the garlic. I added my garlic at this point because it will cook better with the potatoes taking most of the bheat. Pilau usually does not include garlic, but I personally find it tastes alot better with garlic included. Let it simmer
  3. Next add a couple of tablespoons of the freshly made pilau masala and the pre cooked beef. You can use chicken or mutton, but whichever meat you use, make sure it is pre-cooked
  4. Let this simmer for a few minutes and once the potatoes are halfway done, add the rice + water/ stock and let this cook until the rice is done and the potatoes soft.
  5. It will take about 20 minutes to cook. As that is cooking, prep some kachumbari that you will have this with or your favorite salad or as some people enjoy to – with just some sweet bananas.
